Extruder Start for Recycled LDPE and HDPE: Formulations, Linux Controls

Date: April 23, 2024 | Author: Ihor Kryazhev, Extrusion Technology Consultant

1. Technological challenges when switching to 100% secondary raw materials

When processing recycled LDPE and HDPE, quality losses occur mainly in the agglomeration (LDPE) and hot washing with flotation (HDPE) stages. Knowledge of specific parameters (degassing temperature, shear rate, filtration) allows productivity to increase by 35% and scrap to reduce by 20%.

2. Extruder start for LDPE regranulate

  • Pre-agglomeration — bulk density from 50 to 500 kg/m³ (Herbold or NGR)
  • Twin-screw extruder with forced feeding (zones 180-240°C)
  • Vacuum degassing — evaporation surface of 0.5-1.0 m² per t/h
  • Two-stage filtration 300→150 micron (for food 80 micron), Gneuss/Nordson screens

3. Formulation for HDPE with 50% PCR

For household chemical bottles (Persil, Fairy): 50% recyclate from REMONDIS (MFI 0.5, density 0.958) + 50% virgin HDPE + Irganox 1010 (0.2-0.3%) + antistatic agent. For 100% PCR, deodorization is required (vacuum + hot air 120°C) and control of volatiles < 50 ppm.

4. Pelletizer tuning and scrap reduction

  • "Fish eye" (not melted) → increase temperature by 10-15°C or reduce feed
  • Caked pellets → raise water temperature in the bath to 35-40°C, adjust drying
  • Dust (fine fraction) → adjust knife gap (0.1-0.2 mm) and rotor speed (850-1200 rpm)

5. Working with controllers on Embedded Linux

Modern extruders (KraussMaffei, Erema, Battenfeld) are equipped with controllers running Embedded Linux. Typical tasks for a technologist: Diagnose CANopen protocol via SSH access; Tune PID controllers of heating zones via QT-based HMI; Record trends (temperature/pressure/throughput) using gnuplot; Emergency recovery after failures — check file systems, restart systemd, work with U-Boot bootloader.

Real case: After voltage fluctuations at a plant in Bavaria, the controller of an HDPE pelletizing line failed. Remote diagnostics via serial port and reloading U-Boot restored operation within 3 hours without an on-site visit.

6. Examples of successful transition to 100% recyclate

  • mtm plastics (Thuringia): Optimization of agglomeration (450 rpm, 130°C) increased the yield of clear regranulate from 15% to 22%
  • Plant in Bavaria: Two-stage washing with hot caustic (80°C, NaOH 2%) reduced sand content in LDPE from 1.2% to 0.3%
  • Drainage pipe manufacturer (Poland): Formulation of 60% recyclate + 35% virgin + 5% chalk reduced pipe costs by 22% (DIN CERTCO certificate, ring stiffness SN4)

7. Conclusion for extrusion plant owners

Proper line setup and formulation pay for themselves in the first season: reducing scrap from 15% to 3-5% increases margins by 25-30%.

Data April 2024: benchmarking of 12 lines in Germany, Austria and Poland, instructions from KraussMaffei and Erema.
